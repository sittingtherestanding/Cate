Mnemonic devices (pronounced "neh-mohn-icks") are an easy way to remember things.

To use mnemonics, we work to make stories and pictures in your mind.

Your brain can remember pictures better than facts.

There are four rules when making mnemonic stories:

1) Substitution: Picture one item instead of the other

2) Proportion: Try to see the items larger than life

3) Exaggeration: Imagine "millions" of something instead of just one

4) Action: Imagine something crashing, lifting, flying, climbing, etc.
